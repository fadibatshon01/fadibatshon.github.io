The market is a voting machine in the short run and a weighing machine in the long run. Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy when others are fearful. Risk comes from not knowing what you are doing. Forecasting tells us nothing of the future, but tells us much about the forecaster. Compound interest is the most powerful force in the universe. The four most dangerous words in investing are this time it is different. The investor's chief problem, and even his worst enemy, is likely to be himself. Markets can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent. Price is what you pay; value is what you get.

Look at market fluctuations as your friend rather than your enemy; profit from folly rather than participate in it. The intelligent investor is a realist who sells to optimists and buys from pessimists. Time arbitrage is the most underrated form of edge in markets. Patience is undervalued. Conviction is dangerous. Curiosity is the only honest posture. The most contrarian thing one can do is to think clearly when everyone else is panicking, and to act slowly when everyone else is rushing for the exit. Read the tape, read the room, read the data, and then write down what you actually think.

Writing — Fadi Batshon

On this project.

"The bottom line is what's left when the noise clears — when the headline fades, the trade closes, the cycle turns."

The Bottom Line is a working view on markets and economies — short pieces on what's actually moving the price, what the data says, and what the consensus is getting wrong. Less hot takes, more carefully held positions.

The aim is to think clearly in public, build the muscle of reasoning under uncertainty, and accept the cost of being wrong durably. Set in Cormorant Garamond and IBM Plex Sans. Written from La Jolla. Updated whenever there's something worth saying.
