# Notes from the Earnings Tape

A working set of observations from this quarter's prints. Not predictions — descriptions.

- **Beats no longer move stocks.** A 5% EPS beat on consensus is now table stakes. The reaction function has shifted to forward guidance, full stop. Companies that beat but guide flat get sold; companies that miss but raise get bid. The market is pricing the next four quarters, not the one you just reported.

- **Margin compression is the story.** Three quarters in a row, the median S&P 500 operating margin has come in below sell-side estimates. Cost pressure looks structural now, not transitory. Watch the operating-leverage trade unwind across consumer staples.

- **Capex guides are quietly diverging.** Hyperscalers are raising capex; everyone else is cutting. The dispersion between AI-adjacent and AI-distant capex is the widest it's been in a decade. That's both a thesis and a concentration risk.

- **The "soft landing" narrative is doing the work.** Several companies that would have been sold off five years ago for the same print are holding bid because the macro narrative is forgiving. When that narrative breaks, the reprice will be ugly.

The bottom line: the tape this quarter is telling you guidance matters more than the print, margins are the problem, and the macro narrative is doing more work than it should be.
