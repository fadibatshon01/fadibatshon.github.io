# Joining EY for the Summer

This summer I'll be joining EY as a consulting intern. It's the kind of seat I've been working toward since I started thinking seriously about what comes after Rady — and it lines up almost too cleanly with what this blog is supposed to be about.

Consulting, at its best, is the discipline of being asked impossible questions and having to structure an answer anyway. Limited information, a clock, a client who has a real problem and pays you to think clearly about it. That's the same muscle I've been trying to build here — taking ambiguous markets, ambiguous data, ambiguous policy, and forcing them into a clear take I'll defend in writing.

A short list of what I want to learn this summer:

- **How senior consultants frame a problem in the first ten minutes.** The framing is most of the work; everyone can run a model once the question is set.
- **How to write for executives who don't have time for your reasoning chain.** A blog rewards the showing-your-work paragraph. A client deck rewards a headline that's already the answer.
- **How real businesses make capital allocation calls under constraint.** Not the textbook NPV exercise — the version where stakeholders push back, the data is messy, and you have to commit on a timeline.

I'll keep writing here through the summer, though the posts will stay closer to markets and macro than to whatever I'm doing on a project. Client work stays in the client room.

The bottom line: this is the seat I wanted. Excited to find out which of my priors hold up against a real desk.
