# Why I'm Skeptical of the Soft Landing

Every cycle ends with the same dance. The hawks declare victory, the doves declare relief, and the consensus settles on a Goldilocks middle: inflation tamed, growth intact, no recession needed. It's almost never right, and the reason it's almost never right is that the consensus migrates *because* the data has already started to tell a different story.

Three things make me skeptical of the current soft-landing narrative.

## The yield curve dis-inversion

Curves don't invert and then recess. They invert, dis-invert, and *then* recess. The bear-steepening of the past several months is exactly the pattern that preceded the last three downturns. The market is doing what it always does — front-running cuts that haven't happened yet.

## Unemployment ticks up slowly, then quickly

The Sahm rule has triggered or come close to triggering in nearly every state that matters for aggregate consumption. Unemployment doesn't tick up gradually and then stop; it ticks up gradually and then accelerates, because layoffs are reflexive across sectors.

## The consensus moved without an update

When every sell-side strategist who was calling for a hard landing two years ago is now calling for a soft landing with no intermediate revision to their priors, the signal isn't "we got smarter." The signal is "the data forced us to update, and we updated to the most palatable position."

The bottom line: the soft landing is the consensus that lets everyone stay long. That's exactly when I get nervous.
