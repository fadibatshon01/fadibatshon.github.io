# What This Is, and Why

Most market commentary is noise — recycled headlines, reactive takes, the same five charts with a new caption. The bottom line is what's left when that clears: what's actually moving the price, what the data says, and what the consensus is getting wrong.

This is where I'll work that out — short pieces on markets, monetary policy, capital allocation, and the economics underneath the headlines. Sometimes a chart. Sometimes a paragraph that turns into a thesis. Always dated, always accountable.

## What you'll find here

**Markets and money, mostly.** Equities, rates, FX, commodities — wherever the price action is asking a question worth answering. Earnings, sector dynamics, valuation work. Where the consensus has run too far and where it hasn't run far enough.

**Macro and policy, when they matter.** Central bank decisions, fiscal moves, regulatory shifts — but only insofar as they hit markets and the real economy. Less politics-as-spectacle, more policy-as-input-to-the-model.

**Frameworks, not predictions.** I'm more interested in how to think about an asset class than in calling next week's print. The half-life of a good framework is years; the half-life of a hot take is a Tuesday.

## What this isn't

A newsletter. A hot-takes feed. A place to be first or loudest. I'm writing this to build the muscle of reasoning under uncertainty — the same muscle that matters in equity research, consulting, and any analytical seat worth holding.

If something here makes you think, push back, or send a counterargument — that's the whole loop.
